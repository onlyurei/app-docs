Colors of the specs table cells indicate the compatibility of the new tire/wheel in that spec:

  • No color: compatible - Green: improved - Yellow: tolerable - Red: not compatible

Why is it important to keep the replacement tire's overall circumference as close as possible to the original tire's?

Most of the vehicles rely on the tire's revs per mile/kilometer to calculate the vehicle speed, which is the input of various important parts of the vehicle such as speedometer, odometer, ABS, etc.

If the replacement tire's overall circumference has large discrepancy than the original tire's, the tire's revs per mile/kilometer will change by a large amount accordingly, so as the derived vehicle speed. It then affects the accuracy and effectiveness of the aforementioned important parts of the vehicle to the degree beyond the safe point.

Since the tires' overall circumference vary, it doesn't make sense to set an absolute threshold value of the discrepancy in length, instead, percentage is used.

The app's default and recommended value of the maximum tire circumference difference percentage is 1.5.
